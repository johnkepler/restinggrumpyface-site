Small software stands on a lot of shoulders. These are the frameworks, projects, and people that made it all possible.

yaAnnotator

yaAnnotator includes portions of the following open-source software, used under the Apache License 2.0 or the MIT License. Attributions are listed below; the complete license texts are in the project's ThirdPartyNotices.txt.

swift-markdown

The in-app Help is authored in Markdown and rendered with swift-markdown (Apple / swiftlang) — github.com/swiftlang/swift-markdown. Copyright © Apple Inc.

sam2-studio

The Smart Select click-to-segment pipeline is adapted from sam2-studio (Hugging Face) — github.com/huggingface/sam2-studio. Copyright © Hugging Face.

SAM 2.1 (built-in model)

yaAnnotator bundles the SAM 2.1 Tiny Core ML model so Smart Select works out of the box — SAM 2 by Meta FAIR (github.com/facebookresearch/sam2), converted to Core ML by Apple (huggingface.co/apple/coreml-sam2.1-tiny). Copyright © Meta Platforms, Inc.

YOLOv3 (built-in detector)

yaAnnotator bundles the YOLOv3 Core ML detector (Int8-quantized) for the Detect Multiple option in Assist — it finds 80 common object classes. From Apple's Core ML model gallery (huggingface.co/apple/coreml-YOLOv3), originally YOLOv3 by Joseph Redmon et al. (pjreddie/darknet). MIT licensed. Copyright © Apple Inc.

RF-DETR

The RF-DETR detector's output decoder is adapted from RF-DETR (Roboflow) — github.com/roboflow/rf-detr. Copyright © Roboflow, Inc.

Models you supply

Apart from the built-in SAM 2.1 Tiny and YOLOv3 above, yaAnnotator bundles no model weights. Larger Smart Select variants and your own detection models are downloaded by you and loaded at runtime, governed by the usage rights you agree to at download time.
